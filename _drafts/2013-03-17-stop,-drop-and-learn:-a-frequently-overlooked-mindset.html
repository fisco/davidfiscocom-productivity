--- layout: post title: 'Stop, Drop and Learn: A Frequently Overlooked Mindset' date: '2013-03-17T18:59:00.000-04:00' author: David Fisco tags: modified_time: '2013-03-17T18:59:33.421-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-842616179832437348.post-2198617672652978602 --- This article discusses a technique for maximizing performance in any task, by ensuring that you are able to perform all sub-tasks before you begin.

The technique I am going to discuss should strike you as obvious, but in my dealings with learners of all ages, I am surprised at how often these simple instructions are overlooked.  I call the technique "Stop, Drop and Learn", or stated another way, don't forge ahead with anything until you know what you're doing.

Any activity--grand or petit--requires prior knowledge.  Remember when you didn't know how to tie your shoelaces?  Well, I once knew a man in his sixties who hadn't learned that if he double-knotted his shoelaces they had a lower probability of becoming untied throughout the day.  He's someone who would have benefitted from Stop, Drop and Learn.
